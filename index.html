01 — About

A researcher who likes to teach

I'm Abrar Samin, a Computer Science & Engineering graduate from BRAC University, where I finished with a CGPA of 3.97/4.00. My work lives at the intersection of applied machine learning, computer vision, and explainable AI — I care as much about why a model makes a decision as whether it's accurate.

I've co-authored three IEEE publications spanning explainable stroke recognition from facial landmarks and machine-learning approaches to sleep-stage classification from physiological signals. As an Undergraduate Teaching Assistant for Data Structures and Algorithms, I've helped students turn abstract concepts into working intuition — the part of research and engineering I enjoy most.

As a researcher under BRAC University's Research Seed Grant Initiative (RSGI), I'm helping build an AI-powered assistive navigation system that supports safe, independent mobility for blind and low-vision users — combining computer vision with efficient on-device (edge) AI on a low-cost wearable platform.

02 — Research

Research interests

The problems and methods I keep coming back to.

  • Machine Learning

    Designing and evaluating models that learn from real, messy data — with an emphasis on reliability and honest metrics.

  • Computer Vision

    Extracting structure from images and video — facial landmarks, MediaPipe pipelines, and vision for wearables.

  • Explainable AI (XAI)

    Opening the black box — turning predictions into human-interpretable evidence clinicians and users can trust.

  • Embedded / Edge AI

    Running models on-device — low latency, low power, and no cloud dependency for assistive hardware.

  • Assistive Technology

    Human-centered AI that expands independence — starting with safe navigation for blind and low-vision users.

  • Biomedical Signal Processing

    Learning from physiological signals like PPG — sleep staging and health insights from wearable sensors.
